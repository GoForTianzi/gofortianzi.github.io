OYO Japan Mobile Sign-up Mockup

UX & Research

Overview

OYO Japan is a start-up for Ryokan, or budget hotel, booking in Japan. Japanese Ryokans are usually a family-pwned business. They prepare home-made food and usually come with hot springs. I was asked to create a mobile number sign-up optimal flow for the mobile app as an interview test.

My Process

  1. Downloaded the app, looked at app store banners and competitor's app, such as Rakuten Hotel, to understand the business model. Looked at Chinese apps, where mobile sign-ups are most commonly used and have became standardized.
  2. Researched mobile sign-up-screen inspiration on Google and pinterest.
  3. Use case to solidify the flow.
  4. Wireframes in InVision.
  5. Borrowed design elements from Invision UI Kit, Google Material
  6. Iteration: While I am happy with the inline button used for sending passcode in Chinese mobile apps, I do feel that English versions of Chinese apps usually fail to retain the consistency. I changed "Send Code" button to "Verify".
  7. Created interactions to test the flow and made adjustments accordingly.

Mobile-Number-Sign-Up Use Case

A person is planning a trip to Kyoto and he wants to reserve a ryokan for the two. He heard about OYO for having good prices, he dowloads the app...

  • 1.Open the app
  • 2.Learn a bit more about OYO Japan and the app
  • 3.Initiate Sign-up process
  • 4.Input his mobile number
  • 5.Request a one-time passcode
  • 6.Receive a message with a one-time passcode
  • 7.Input the passcode
  • 8.Agree with privacy policy
  • 9.Finish sign-up
